A Brief Explanation

I grew up eating every possible sugar cereal. From the old standby Froot Loops to the rare editions of Cap'n'Crunch's Oops! All Berries, we had them all at my house. Chip, the burgling dog Cookie Crisp asked from my TV, "COOKIES? For BREAKFAST?" And I could answer proudly, "Yes, old friend. But only when we're out of Rice Krispies Treats Cereal."

However, there was one cereal that was forbidden in my household: Lucky Charms. My parents drew the line at fake marshmallows, and so Lucky the Leprechaun, like pork and shellfish, was banned from the pantry.

Fortunately, my friend Alex ALWAYS had Lucky Charms at his house. The morning after sleep-overs, a bowl of the good stuff was always in order. This game is about those bowls.

    Instructions:
  • Mouse down to move stuff about with your spoon.
  • Click a cereal bit to eat it and everything it's glommed on to




What Else?

This is the first game that I made by myself. I'm incredibly fortunate to have had food on my table throughout my entire life. My easy-going relationship with food is what allowed me to make Yucky Charms.

Hopefully, you too can play with your food- you can save all the marshmallows for the end of the bowl, separate m&m's by color before eating, and chew around the middle of carrots. Unfortunately, that's not nearly always the case in America where hunger still exists for 1 in 6 Americans (THAT'S 49 MILLION PEOPLE).

Donating to this food drive will help solve a real problem for real families. If you have the means to do so, I hope you help out.

Go to the Yucky Charms Food Drive here and help spread the word with all your socialness.
